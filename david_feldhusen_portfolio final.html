02 — My journey

✈️ Three countries, one story

I've always loved travelling — I'm genuinely lucky to have been to a lot of places in my life. But going to Ghana and Canada wasn't just travel. Both trips had a purpose, and that changed everything. These weren't holidays. They were chapters that shaped who I am — and the experiences I came back with are ones I carry into everything I do.

🇩🇪
Germany
🏠 Home · 📚 Foundation · 💼 First steps

Germany is where everything started. I grew up in Lüneburg, a small city in northern Germany, and went through my entire school career at Gymnasium Oedeme. From primary school through to my Abitur, this was where I built the academic foundation that everything else sits on.

What makes my school years stand out is the bilingual science programme — for four years, from grades 7 to 10, all natural science subjects were taught entirely in English. That early exposure to academic English gave me a head start that went well beyond what most language courses can offer. I also chose social sciences as my Abitur specialisation, covering politics, economics and society — which turned out to be a direct runway into Business Management.

In between finishing school and starting university, I joined the team at EDEKA Bergmann in Lüneburg. I worked across warehouse operations, the checkout, and general customer support — nothing glamorous, but it taught me a lot about showing up reliably, staying calm under pressure, and working as part of a real team that depends on each other to get things done.

I'm now studying European Business Management at Technische Hochschule Wildau near Berlin, where I'm specialising in Digital Marketing from the 4th semester onwards. I've also had the chance to work on a real client project — developing a market-entry and sales strategy for Maile GmbH, a self-service payment systems company. That kind of applied work, where you're delivering for an actual business, is where I feel most at home.

Ghana
🤝 Volunteering · 🏀 Teaching · 💛 Community

Ghana changed me. That's really the simplest way to put it. I went there through the German Red Cross (DRK) and the Agricultural and Rural Development Association (ARA) to volunteer as an assistant PE teacher — and what I came back with was something I couldn't have anticipated before I left.

Being in a classroom, earning the trust of students, actually teaching — it was one of the most meaningful things I've ever done. There's something about standing in front of a group of kids who are genuinely curious and full of energy that stays with you. I fell in love with it. And I fell in love with Ghana itself — the warmth of the people, the way communities work together, the way life feels more vivid and more present there than almost anywhere else I've been.

I came back a different person. Not in a way that's easy to put into words, but in the way you can only become when you've lived somewhere that challenges your assumptions about everything — about how people connect, about what matters, about what you're actually capable of. Ghana gave me that. I'm genuinely grateful for every day I spent there, and it's a country I carry with me.

Canada
🏙️ Vancouver · 🍁 Montréal · 🌆 Toronto · 📝 C1 English

My first time in Canada was in 2022, and honestly, I wasn't sure what to expect. I'd signed up for an intensive English course at EC English School in Vancouver, and what started as a language programme turned into something much bigger. Vancouver got under my skin immediately — the mix of cultures, the energy of the city, the feeling that you could be anyone there. I passed my C1 English certificate 📜 in July 2022, but more than the qualification, I came away genuinely fluent and confident in English in a way that classroom learning in Germany hadn't quite managed.

I came back to Canada in 2024, after finishing school, because I wanted more of it. This time I stayed longer, worked, and actually lived there rather than just studying. I spent time working across multiple bars in Vancouver as a waiter and bartender — long shifts, fast pace, a constant mix of people from everywhere. You learn a lot about communication, pressure, and keeping your cool when things get chaotic.

Later I spent a month each in Montréal and Toronto. Montréal was a completely different experience — bilingual, European in feel, a city that takes its culture seriously. Toronto was almost the opposite: huge, fast, cosmopolitan in the most genuine sense. Both added something that Vancouver hadn't, and together they gave me a much fuller picture of what Canada actually is beyond the west coast postcard.

04 — Skills

🛠️ What I bring

The skill I'm probably most proud of is one that doesn't fit neatly on a CV: genuine intercultural communication. Not the kind you get from a course, but the kind that comes from actually being in rooms — a school in Ghana, a bar in Vancouver, a university in Berlin — where the people around you think and communicate completely differently from how you grew up. You learn to adapt, to listen properly, and to read situations quickly. That's something I bring to every team I'm part of.

Working at EDEKA taught me what reliability and organisation actually look like in practice. Showing up on time, knowing your role, doing the unglamorous parts well — things like warehouse logistics, cashier operations and customer support don't sound impressive but they build a work ethic that carries into everything else. I'm someone who takes that kind of responsibility seriously.

On the technical side, I'm confident across the full MS Office suite — Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Teams — and I've developed solid academic writing skills through my university work. I'm also comfortable with AI tools as genuine productivity tools rather than shortcuts, and I have hands-on experience in video editing from personal projects. I pick up new software and platforms quickly, which has always been one of my more useful traits.

Through the Digital Marketing specialisation at TH Wildau and the real-world collaboration with Maile GmbH, I've built practical knowledge in marketing strategy, online channels and data-driven decision making. I'm drawn to work where you can see the impact of what you're doing — strategy that actually gets implemented, not just presented.

Across all of it, the thing people usually notice first is that I'm a fast learner. Every new environment — Ghana, Canada, university — has confirmed the same thing: I get comfortable quickly, I ask the right questions, and I start contributing before most people expect me to. I'm also genuinely a team player: I've worked in enough different group settings by now to know that the best outcomes come from good communication and mutual respect, not from trying to stand out at the expense of others.

06 — Inspirations

💡 What inspired this

I wanted this portfolio to feel like me — not like a template someone filled in. The starting point was my own story and the three very different places that shaped it. From there, I looked at portfolios that had a clear theme running through everything: a colour, a structure, an idea that made you actually feel something rather than just scroll past bullet points.

What I found is that the best portfolios don't just list experience — they show you who the person is. That's what I was going for here. Every design choice, every section, every colour shift is meant to reflect a different part of the journey.

🌍
The three-country structure
Germany, Ghana and Canada aren't just places on a CV — they're genuinely different chapters, each with its own character and lessons. I wanted that to be immediately obvious when you look at the Journey section, so each country gets its own colour, tone and story.
🎨
Design that moves like the story
The colour palette shifts as you move through the site — violet for About, green for Journey, amber for Education, sky blue for Skills — so you feel the change in context before you even read a word. The smooth page transitions and animated elements mirror the idea of someone who keeps moving forward.
📸
Pictures that prove the story
Anyone can write "lived in Ghana" on a CV. Photos make it real. The gallery is there to turn a list of experiences into something tangible — actual moments from actual places, not stock imagery. That's what makes a portfolio memorable.
